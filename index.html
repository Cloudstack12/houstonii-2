Understanding the Coverage of Home Health Services By Medicare


Home health services are essential for people who need medical attention but prefer to receive care in their homes. These services can help improve patients' quality of life with chronic illnesses, disabilities, or those recovering from surgery. Medicare, a federal health insurance program for people 65 or older, provides coverage for a wide range of home health services.

Understanding the coverage offered by Medicare is crucial for beneficiaries who may need these services, as it can impact their healthcare expenses and overall well-being. In this blog, we will explore the definition of home health services and discuss the importance of understanding Medicare coverage. We will also provide an overview of the types of home health services covered by Medicare, eligibility criteria, coverage limits, and tips for finding a Medicare-certified home health agency.

Eligibility for Medicare Coverage

To be eligible for Medicare coverage of home health services, beneficiaries must meet specific criteria. First, they must be enrolled in Medicare Part A and Part B. Second; they must require skilled nursing care or therapy services ordered by a physician and provided by a Medicare-certified home health agency. Third, they must be homebound, meaning they can only leave their home with assistance, or it would be medically unadvisable.


Types of Medicare Coverage

Medicare offers two types of coverage for home health services: Part A and Part B.

  • Part A Coverage: Medicare Part A covers home health services deemed medically necessary and ordered by a physician. This includes skilled nursing care, physical therapy, speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, medical social services, and home health aide services. Part A also covers medical equipment and supplies, such as wheelchairs, walkers, and oxygen equipment.

  • Part B Coverage: Medicare Part B covers medically necessary outpatient services, including physical therapy, speech-language pathology services, and occupational therapy. Part B also covers specific medical equipment and supplies considered durable (DME), such as walkers, wheelchairs, and oxygen equipment.

It is important to note that Part B coverage for home health services is subject to certain limits, including the frequency and duration of services and prior authorization requirements. Additionally, depending on the type of service provided, beneficiaries may be responsible for cost-sharing, such as co-payments and deductibles.


Understanding the eligibility criteria and types of coverage offered by Medicare for home health services is crucial for beneficiaries who may need these services. Beneficiaries should also be aware of any limits on coverage and cost-sharing requirements to ensure they receive the appropriate care and minimize out-of-pocket expenses.

Covered Home Health Services

Medicare offers coverage for a wide range of home health services deemed medically necessary and ordered by a physician. These services can help beneficiaries receive the care they need in the comfort of their own homes. Here are the different types of covered home health services under Medicare:

  • Skilled Nursing Care: Skilled nursing care is provided by licensed nurses who can perform various services, such as wound care, medication management, and administering injections. Professional nursing care can also involve educating patients and their caregivers on proper care techniques and medication management.

  • Physical Therapy: Physical therapy involves exercise, manual therapy, and other techniques to help patients regain strength, mobility, and flexibility. Physical therapy can also help manage pain and prevent falls, particularly in elderly patients.

  • Speech-Language Pathology Services: Speech-language pathology services involve diagnosing and treating speech, language, and swallowing disorders. In addition, these services can help patients who have suffered a stroke, have a neurological disorder, or have difficulty communicating due to an injury or illness.

  • Occupational Therapy: Occupational therapy focuses on helping patients perform daily tasks and activities, such as bathing, dressing, and cooking. This type of therapy can also involve assisting patients with mobility issues and providing them with adaptive equipment to help them function independently.

  • Medical Social Services: Medical social services provide counseling and support to patients and their families, particularly those struggling with social, emotional, or financial issues related to their healthcare needs.

  • Home Health Aide Services: Home health aide services involve providing personal care and assistance with activities of daily living, such as bathing, dressing, and eating. Home health aides can also assist with light housekeeping tasks and meal preparation.

  • Medical Equipment and Supplies: Medicare also covers medical equipment and supplies that are deemed medically necessary for home health services. This can include oxygen equipment, hospital beds, and wheelchairs.

Medicare offers coverage for a wide range of home health services that can help beneficiaries receive the care they need in the comfort of their own homes. However, beneficiaries must know the covered services to ensure appropriate care and minimize out-of-pocket expenses.


Limits on Coverage

While Medicare does offer coverage for various home health services, there are limits to the range that beneficiaries should be aware of. Here are some of the limitations on coverage for home health services under Medicare:


Frequency and Duration of Services

Under Medicare Part A, beneficiaries can receive unlimited visits from a skilled nursing care provider, physical therapist, or speech-language pathologist. However, there are limits on the duration of coverage for these services. Beneficiaries can receive up to 100 days of skilled nursing care and physical therapy per benefit period. Speech-language pathology services are covered separately, with no limit on the number of visits or duration of coverage.


Under Medicare Part B, beneficiaries are subject to limits on the frequency and duration of services for physical therapy, occupational therapy, and speech-language pathology services. For example, beneficiaries can receive up to 20 visits yearly for material and occupational therapy.


Prior Authorization Requirements

Certain home health services under Medicare Part B require prior authorization before being covered. This means that the home health agency must obtain approval from Medicare before providing the service. Services that require prior permission may include specific medical equipment and supplies and certain types of therapy services.


Cost-Sharing

Beneficiaries may be responsible for cost-sharing, such as co-payments and deductibles, depending on the type of service provided. For home health services under Medicare Part A, beneficiaries typically do not have any cost-sharing responsibilities. However, beneficiaries may be responsible for 20% of the Medicare-approved amount for services covered under Medicare Part B.


Medicare offers home health services coverage, beneficiaries should be aware of the coverage limits. These limits may include restrictions on the frequency and duration of services, prior authorization requirements, and cost-sharing responsibilities. Understanding these limits can help beneficiaries receive appropriate care and minimize out-of-pocket expenses.


Finding a Medicare-Certified Home Health Agency

If you are a Medicare beneficiary needing home health services, it is essential to find a Medicare-certified agency to ensure that Medicare covers your services. Here are some tips for finding a Medicare-certified home health agency:

  • Use the Medicare Home Health Compare Tool: The Medicare Home Health Compare tool is an online database that allows beneficiaries to search for and compare home health agencies based on quality ratings, services offered, and location. In addition, the tool provides information on each agency's performance in areas such as patient care, safety, and patient satisfaction.

  • Ask for Referrals: Ask your physician, hospital discharge planner, or other healthcare professionals for referrals to Medicare-certified home health agencies in your area. They may have experience working with certain agencies and can offer recommendations based on their knowledge and experience.

  • Check the Agency's Certification Status: You can verify an agency's certification status by checking the Medicare Certification and Survey Provider Enhanced Reporting (CASPER) system. This system provides information on the agency's compliance with Medicare regulations and any history of deficiencies or violations.

Questions to Ask When Choosing an Agency

When choosing a Medicare-certified home health agency, consider asking the following questions:

  • What specific services does the agency offer?
  • Does Medicare cover the agency's services?
  • What is the agency's experience providing care for patients with my condition?
  • What is the agency's quality rating according to Medicare Home Health Compare?
  • Is the agency licensed and insured?
  • Are the agency's staff members approved and trained in their respective fields?
  • How does the agency handle emergencies?
  • What is the agency's process for creating a care plan?
  • What is the agency's process for communication with the patient and their family?

Finding a Medicare-certified home health agency is crucial to ensure that Medicare covers your services. Utilize online tools such as Medicare Home Health Compare, ask for referrals, and check an agency's certification status to find a reputable agency. When choosing an agency, ask relevant questions to ensure you receive appropriate care and quality services.

As a Medicare beneficiary, knowing your coverage and rights is essential. This includes understanding the types of services covered, the limits on coverage, and the process for accessing and receiving care. By staying informed and advocating for yourself, you can receive the appropriate care and support to maintain your health and well-being.
